# ROI Analysis — Ember & Oak Brand Identity

## Summary Metrics
- **Time Investment:**  
  - One-shot prompting: 15 minutes  
  - Workflow approach: 120 minutes  
  - Delta: +105 minutes (8x time)

- **Quality Score (self-assessed + stakeholder feedback):**  
  - One-shot prompting: 4/10 — generic, unusable without rework.  
  - Workflow approach: 9/10 — adopted unchanged for launch assets.  
  - Improvement: +5 points (2.25x quality when normalized on a 0–10 scale).

- **Reusability:**  
  - One-shot: None — output lives and dies in a single prompt thread.  
  - Workflow: High — positioning pillars, visual kit, and messaging sequences archived for future launches; derivatives already reused for two client projects.

## Detailed Breakdown

### Time vs. Output Value
- The workflow consumes ~2 hours across four specialists, but each section feeds a launch-ready asset.  
- One-shot prompt produces a summary that still requires full strategic work afterward, resulting in duplicated effort.

### Quality & Strategic Alignment
- Workflow output ties every decision to research insights and measurable outcomes. Stakeholders approved without additional workshops.  
- One-shot result failed to address real objections or differentiation; it risked undermining credibility if shipped as-is.

### Reusable Artifacts
- Workflow created:  
  - Positioning framework reusable for subscription products.  
  - Visual system kit integrated into design library.  
  - Messaging playbook stored in templates folder.  
  - Decision log referencing why certain directions were chosen, accelerating future iterations.
- One-shot produced nothing reusable; team would start from scratch next time.

## When to Choose Each Approach
- **Use the workflow when:** Stakeholders need conviction, launch assets must roll out across multiple channels, or you expect to reuse the identity pattern.  
- **Use the one-shot when:** You need a fast sketch to unblock internal brainstorming with the expectation of deeper work later.

## Process Acceleration Over Time
- After codifying the workflow, subsequent brand identity projects now take ~90 minutes because insights, prompts, and artifact templates are pre-built.  
- The initial 2-hour investment pays back after a single reuse.
