## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Project Objectives and Success Criteria
- **Primary Objective:** Launch AI-Staff-HQ publicly as “cognitive infrastructure as code,” showcasing personal system while guiding operators to build their own.
- **Success Criteria:**
  - □ Acquire 1,000 qualified repository visitors within first 14 days  
  - □ Secure 25 inbound conversations with operators/builders for follow-up  
  - □ Obtain 3 published case-study commitments from early adopters  
  - □ Establish repeatable onboarding workflow captured in knowledge base
- **Business Impact:** Positions the framework as a methodology, not a product; opens advisory pipeline; seeds future community contributions.

### 1.2 Scope Definition and Deliverables
- **In Scope:**  
  - □ Repository polish (README reframing, PHILOSOPHY.md, examples library)  
  - □ Launch messaging suite (website updates, email sequence, social stories)  
  - □ Live workshop outline demonstrating workflow orchestration  
  - □ Measurement dashboard for real-time launch metrics
- **Out of Scope:**  
  - □ Paid acquisition campaigns  
  - □ Full documentation localization  
  - □ Software platform integrations beyond GitHub hosting
- **Dependencies:**  
  - □ Specialist example completion (Copywriter, Brand Strategist, Data Analyst)  
  - □ Workflow transcripts from in-flight client projects  
  - □ Scheduling availability for live workshop (within launch week)

### 1.3 Timeline & Milestones
- **Launch Window:** 6 weeks (planning) + 2 weeks (post-launch amplification)  
- **Key Milestones:**  
  - Week 1: Narrative alignment (Brand Strategist) + positioning sign-off  
  - Week 2: Example specialists + project briefs drafted  
  - Week 3: Workflow transcripts and before/after assets live  
  - Week 4: Website refresh + email nurture sequence completed  
  - Week 5: Live workshop dry run; measurement dashboard validated  
  - Week 6: Launch week — publish repo update, send announcements, host workshop  
  - Week 7–8: Post-launch follow-up, collect case-study commitments, finalize retrospective

### 1.4 Budget & Resource Allocation
- **Budget:** $3,500 soft budget (tools, design polish, advertising cushion)  
- **Allocation:**  
  - $1,500: Design/UI support for workshop visuals (allocated but unused — in-house)  
  - $1,000: Marketing automation + analytics tooling upgrades  
  - $500: Contingency  
  - $500: Live event software + transcription

## 2. Strategic Context

### 2.1 Audience & Stakeholders
- **Primary:** Directors/VPs of Operations at venture-backed companies designing AI systems.  
- **Secondary:** Independent operators and product leaders building internal AI teams.  
- **Key Motivations:** Replace prompt roulette with orchestrated workflows; build AI capabilities without hiring immediately.  
- **Objections to Tackle:**  
  - “This looks like your personal system—can it work for us?”  
  - “We don’t have time to learn a massive documentation set.”  
  - “Will LLM shifts make this obsolete?”

### 2.2 Competitive & Comparative Landscape
- **Tier 1:** AI prompt libraries marketed as turnkey solutions; narrative threat is “download prompts, get instant value.”  
- **Tier 2:** Enterprise workflow platforms (e.g., automation suites) — not direct competitors but frame the baseline for structured processes.  
- **Positioning Counterpoint:** Emphasize methodology, context discipline, and customizable workforce—no “prompt pack” pitch.  
- **Evidence:** Operator interview snippets, before/after workflow comparisons, ROI metrics from internal experiments.

### 2.3 Narrative & Messaging Pillars
1. **Infrastructure, not prompts:** Show disciplined architecture.  
2. **Personal system openly shared:** Honesty about incompleteness builds trust.  
3. **Actionable starting paths:** Provide one specialist, one template, one workflow entry point.  
4. **Proof through transcripts:** Demonstrate real prompts, handoffs, and decisions—not theoretical outcomes.

## 3. Experience Strategy & Journey

### 3.1 Customer Journey Stages
1. **Discover:** Social thread + newsletter highlight drives to updated README.  
2. **Evaluate:** Visitors explore examples directory and PHILOSOPHY.md to understand rationale.  
3. **Activate:** Operators clone repo, follow “one specialist/one template” workflow.  
4. **Expand:** Join follow-up workshop, request advisory call, contribute specialist back.

### 3.2 Key Experience Moments
- **Moment 1:** README reframing ensuring expectations (methodology, not turnkey).  
- **Moment 2:** Examples directory delivering concrete proof of system in action.  
- **Moment 3:** Live workshop showing orchestration between specialists.  
- **Moment 4:** Follow-up nurture sequence sharing ROI data and inviting collaboration.

### 3.3 Friction Points & Mitigations
- **Overwhelm:** Provide “Start here” checklist + minimal path; mitigate with Chief of Staff curated workflow.  
- **Skepticism:** Publish transcripts with real prompts; include candid retrospectives.  
- **Context loss:** Data Analyst maintains digestible metrics for ongoing iteration, ensuring follow-up content stays relevant.

## 4. Specialist Assignments & Governance
- **Chief of Staff:** Owns program management, ensures documentation sequence, runs retrospectives.  
- **Brand Strategist:** Crafts positioning, narrative systems, and experience map; approves all messaging.  
- **Copywriter:** Produces README reframing, example intros, launch emails, social narratives.  
- **Data Analyst:** Builds measurement dashboard (traffic, engagement, conversions) + monitors launch signals.  
- **Automation Specialist:** Automates newsletter send, sets up tracking, ensures repo cloning instructions are frictionless.  
- **Art Director (support):** Refreshes visuals for README and workshop deck.  
- **Founder/Operator (me):** Provides raw interview notes, story selection, final go/no-go decisions.

### Governance Cadence
- Weekly launch stand-up led by Chief of Staff.  
- Daily async check-ins during launch week via shared command center doc.  
- Immediate escalation path: Chief of Staff → Founder when blockers threaten timeline >24 hours.

## 5. Communications Plan

### Channels & Assets
- **Owned:**  
  - Newsletter (pre-launch teaser, launch day, 72-hour follow-up)  
  - Website hero update + examples landing page  
  - Workshop (live + replay)  
- **Earned:**  
  - Outreach to existing operator network for shares/testimonials  
  - Community forums (excerpts linking back to repo)
- **Social:**  
  - LinkedIn thought-leadership thread (focus on methodology)  
  - Twitter/X teaser showing before/after comparison  
  - Short-form video clip (behind-the-scenes of workflow transcript)

### Messaging Sequencing
1. **Teaser (T-7 days):** “Personal AI operating system is going public—here’s why.”  
2. **Launch Day (T0):** “AI-Staff-HQ is live — start with one specialist, one template, one workflow.”  
3. **72-hour Follow-up:** Share transcript excerpts + ROI data; invite to workshop.  
4. **Workshop Recap (T+5 days):** Highlight key learnings; call for community contributions.

## 6. Measurement & ROI

### Core Metrics
- **Acquisition:** Unique repo visitors, README dwell time, clone rate.  
- **Engagement:** Newsletter CTR, workshop registrations/attendance, transcript downloads.  
- **Conversion:** Advisory call requests, community contribution offers, follow-up pipeline value.  
- **Feedback:** Qualitative testimonials, GitHub issues/prs with improvement suggestions.

### Dashboard & Ownership
- Data Analyst builds Looker Studio dashboard refreshed hourly launch week.  
- Chief of Staff reviews metrics daily, flags anomalies.  
- Post-launch review at week 8 includes ROI assessment vs time/cost investment.

### Baseline vs Targets
- Baseline repo traffic: 150 visits/week → Target 500 visits/week post-launch.  
- Baseline advisory pipeline: 2/month → Target 6/month after launch.  
- Target ROI: 3x advisory revenue vs launch spend within 60 days.

## 7. Risks & Contingencies

### Top Risks
1. **Perception of incompleteness:** Mitigate by publishing PHILOSOPHY.md explaining conscious scope decisions.  
2. **Context overload:** Provide curated starting path and checklist; Chief of Staff monitors support requests.  
3. **Workflow transcript delays:** Schedule buffer week; if second transcript slips, publish blog post deconstructing single workflow instead.

### Contingency Actions
- Maintain fallback content (Case Study Lite) if before/after asset not ready.  
- Have Automation Specialist pre-build manual outreach list in case newsletter platform throttles.  
- Prepare clarifying FAQ to address “Can I just download prompts?” pushback.

## Retrospective Notes

**What worked:**
- Leaning on the Brand Strategist for narrative guardrails prevented messaging drift across README, examples, and emails.  
- Data Analyst’s real-time dashboard surfaced which channels were converting (newsletter + LinkedIn) so we doubled down mid-week.  
- Publishing candid transcripts built trust—operators quoted specific decision points during follow-up calls.

**What I'd change:**
- Should have engaged Automation Specialist earlier to templatize the workshop follow-up emails; manual sends slowed response time.  
- Underestimated time required for before/after asset polish; next time lock visuals two weeks earlier.

**Time investment:** ~62 hours across six weeks (core team) plus 8 hours post-launch; saved ~15 hours compared to prior launches by reusing checklists and governance cadences.

**Outcomes:**  
- 1,340 qualified visitors in two weeks; 31 advisory conversations booked.  
- Three early adopters agreed to document success stories.  
- Internal workflow now codified for future open-source drops.

**Would reuse this pattern:** Yes — with earlier automation planning and a pre-booked design sprint for before/after visuals.
