## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Project Objectives and Success Criteria
- **Primary Objective:** Publish a 1,200-word blog post that reframes AI prompt tinkering as systems design, supporting the public release narrative.
- **Success Criteria:**
  - □ 35% click-through from newsletter send announcing the article  
  - □ 5 qualitative responses from operators or founders within 72 hours  
  - □ Internal reference for future positioning updates (logged in knowledge base)
- **Business Impact:** Sharpens the repository’s POV ahead of the productized release window and builds authority with operators designing AI teams.

### 1.2 Scope Definition and Deliverable Specifications
- **In Scope:**  
  - □ Long-form article (1,100–1,300 words)  
  - □ Two pull quotes for social distribution  
  - □ Internal checklist of talking points for future webinars
- **Out of Scope:**  
  - □ SEO overhaul or keyword gap analysis  
  - □ Paid acquisition assets  
  - □ Video or audio companion content
- **Dependencies:**  
  - □ Market Analyst summary of recent operator interview themes  
  - □ Brand Strategist narrative spine for “systems thinking” pillar  
  - □ Copywriter availability for two revision cycles

### 1.3 Timeline and Milestone Planning
- **Project Duration:** 5 days  
- **Milestones:**  
  - □ Day 1: Audience insight synthesis (Market Analyst)  
  - □ Day 2: Narrative outline approval (Brand Strategist + me)  
  - □ Day 3–4: Draft + revisions (Copywriter)  
  - □ Day 5: QA + publish (Chief of Staff coordination)

## 2. Strategic Context

### 2.1 Audience Snapshot
- **Primary Stakeholder:** Directors of Operations at seed–Series A startups adopting AI workflows.  
- **Pain Points:** Fragmented prompt experiments, no durable knowledge sharing, leadership skepticism about AI ROI.  
- **Desired Outcome:** A mental model for turning ad hoc prompting into reusable systems they can pitch internally.

### 2.2 ~~Competitive Landscape and Positioning Analysis~~
*Skipped: no competing content to analyze for this single thought-leadership piece.*

### 2.3 Narrative Angle
- Core theme: “Systems thinking is the missing layer between prompts and leverage.”  
- Proof points pulled from internal project retrospectives where structured workflows produced better output with fewer tokens.  
- Tone: Pragmatic mentor — authoritative but accessible.

---

## 3. Specialist Assignments & Handoffs
- **Chief of Staff:** Owns orchestration, timeline enforcement, and final QA.  
- **Market Analyst:** Condense 6 operator interview notes into a two-page insight brief.  
- **Brand Strategist:** Translate insights into narrative spine + key proof points.  
- **Copywriter:** Produce draft and revisions, ensure pull quotes align with hero message.  
- **Prompt Engineer (support):** Spot-check prompts used during draft to maintain context window discipline.

---

## 4. Workflow Plan
1. Chief of Staff triggers Market Analyst to deliver insight synthesis (2-hour SLA).  
2. Brand Strategist converts synthesis into messaging ladder; shares with Copywriter.  
3. Copywriter drafts article, embedding systems-thinking framework and highlighting real workflows.  
4. Chief of Staff reviews for alignment with release talking points, flags data references for accuracy.  
5. Publish to knowledge base + newsletter; log assets for future reuse.

---

## 5. Measurement & Feedback Loop
- **Primary Metrics:** Newsletter CTR, qualitative replies, time-on-page.  
- **Instrumentation:**  
  - Automation Specialist adds UTM tracking for newsletter send.  
  - Chief of Staff sets 72-hour follow-up to collect qualitative feedback.  
  - Data Analyst reviews engagement in weekly metrics sync to determine if follow-up content warranted.

---

## Retrospective Notes

**What worked:**
- Having Market Analyst synthesize interviews first kept the article anchored in operator language.
- Narrative spine from Brand Strategist prevented the draft from drifting into product marketing.
- Two revision cycles were sufficient because proof points were pre-approved.

**What I'd change:**
- Should have slotted time with Automation Specialist earlier to prep social snippets; ended up pulling quotes manually post-launch.
- A lightweight competitive scan might have revealed a partner cross-link opportunity.

**Time investment:** 5.5 hours total across specialists; saved roughly 2 hours compared to starting copy without aligned insights.

**Would reuse this pattern:** Yes — ideal for any single-asset thought-leadership piece where interviews already exist.
