# 🚀 Autonomous Content Creation Workflow

> An autonomous workflow for generating a blog post, from idea to final draft.

## 🎯 Workflow Overview

**Purpose**: To autonomously generate a high-quality, SEO-optimized blog post with minimal user intervention.  
**Trigger**: A user request for a new blog post on a specific topic.  
**Facilitation**: This is an autonomous workflow, primarily driven by the Chief of Staff.  
**Deliverables**: A final draft of a blog post with accompanying visual assets, ready for publication.

---

## 🔄 Workflow Steps

### **Phase 1: Initialization (Chief of Staff)**

1.  **Receive Request**: The Chief of Staff receives a request from the user for a new blog post on a specific topic.
2.  **Activate Specialists**: The Chief of Staff activates the `SEO Specialist`, `Copywriter`, and `Art Director` to begin the content creation process.

### **Phase 2: SEO & Content Brief (SEO Specialist)**

1.  **Keyword Research**: The `SEO Specialist` conducts keyword research on the given topic to identify primary and secondary keywords.
2.  **Content Brief**: The `SEO Specialist` creates a content brief that includes the target audience, keywords, and a recommended structure for the blog post.

### **Phase 3: Content Creation (Copywriter & Art Director)**

1.  **Drafting**: The `Copywriter` writes a full draft of the blog post based on the content brief.
2.  **Visuals**: The `Art Director` creates visual assets for the blog post, including a hero image and in-body graphics.

### **Phase 4: Review & Refinement (Chief of Staff)**

1.  **Internal Review**: The Chief of Staff reviews the draft and visuals to ensure they meet quality standards.
2.  **Revisions**: If necessary, the Chief of Staff requests revisions from the `Copywriter` and `Art Director`.

### **Phase 5: Finalization**

1.  **Final Draft**: The Chief of Staff assembles the final draft of the blog post with the visual assets.
2.  **Presentation**: The final draft is presented to the user for approval.

### **Phase 6: User Approval**

1.  **User Review**: The user reviews the final draft and can either approve it or request changes.
2.  **Approval**: If the user approves the draft, the workflow is complete.
